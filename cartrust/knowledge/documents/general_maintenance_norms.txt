GENERAL USED CAR MAINTENANCE NORMS — INDIA
Last updated: May 2026
Aligned with Indian driving conditions, fuel quality, climate (monsoon, dust,
heat) and the realities of mixed authorised service center + local garage usage.

NOTE: All intervals assume mixed city/highway driving and Indian climate.
For pure city / stop-and-go traffic, halve the upper bound. For heavy
highway use, intervals can be extended by 20–30%. Costs are 2026 estimates
at authorised service centers (ASC); local garages typically 40–60% lower.

=======================================================================
SECTION 1 — ENGINE OIL & FILTER
=======================================================================

ENGINE OIL CHANGE INTERVAL
- Naturally aspirated petrol (Maruti / Hyundai entry):  5,000 – 7,500 km / 12 months
- Naturally aspirated petrol (mid-segment):             7,500 – 10,000 km / 12 months
- Diesel (common-rail, BS6):                            7,500 – 10,000 km / 12 months
- Turbo petrol (1.0L / 1.2L / 1.5L):                    5,000 – 7,500 km / 12 months
- Turbo diesel (e.g. Creta CRDi, Harrier 2.0):          10,000 – 15,000 km / 12 months
- High-performance / luxury (BMW / Audi / Mercedes):    10,000 – 15,000 km / 12 months
- CNG / dual-fuel:                                      5,000 – 7,000 km (lean burn = more wear)
- CarTrust default estimation:                          5,500 km per oil change

OIL FILTER & DRAIN PLUG WASHER
- Replace with EVERY oil change (never reuse)
- Cost (filter + washer): Rs. 250 – 800 mainstream; Rs. 1,500 – 4,000 luxury

OIL GRADE BY CLIMATE
- Tropical India (most cities): 5W-30 or 5W-40 fully synthetic
- Hill stations / cold north (winter): 0W-30 or 5W-30
- Very high-mileage vehicles (>1.5L km): 10W-40 (thicker)

OIL CONSUMPTION RED FLAGS
- >0.5 litre per 1,000 km consumption → piston ring wear / valve seal failure
- Blue smoke on cold start → valve seal leakage (Rs. 8,000–25,000 repair)
- Blue smoke under acceleration → piston ring wear (Rs. 25,000–80,000 engine rebuild)
- Milky / coffee-coloured oil on dipstick → head gasket failure / coolant in oil
  (Rs. 25,000–1,50,000 — DO NOT BUY this car)

OIL CHANGE COST RANGES (per service at ASC, May 2026)
- Hatchback (Alto / WagonR / Swift):     Rs. 2,500 – 4,500
- Premium hatch / Compact sedan:          Rs. 3,500 – 6,500
- Compact SUV (Creta / Seltos / Nexon):   Rs. 4,500 – 8,500
- Mid-size SUV (XUV700 / Harrier):        Rs. 6,000 – 11,000
- Full-size SUV (Fortuner / Endeavour):   Rs. 8,000 – 14,000
- Luxury (BMW / Mercedes / Audi):         Rs. 14,000 – 35,000

=======================================================================
SECTION 2 — TYRES
=======================================================================

TYRE REPLACEMENT INTERVAL
- Budget tyres (Apollo / MRF entry) on city roads:    35,000 – 45,000 km
- Mid-range tyres (Bridgestone / Yokohama) mixed:      40,000 – 55,000 km
- Premium tyres (Michelin / Continental):              50,000 – 65,000 km
- Performance tyres (luxury / SUV):                    30,000 – 45,000 km (softer compound)
- CarTrust estimation benchmark:                       40,000 km per set
- Maximum age regardless of tread: 6 years from DOT date (rubber hardens)

TREAD DEPTH MINIMUMS (Indian Road Safety)
- Legal minimum: 1.6 mm across tread
- Replace at: 2 mm (under 2 mm = aquaplaning risk in monsoon)
- New tyre depth: 7–8 mm
- Tread Wear Indicator (TWI) bars: When flush with tread, replace immediately

WHEEL ALIGNMENT & BALANCING
- Frequency: Every 10,000 km or after pothole hit
- Cost: Rs. 600 – 1,800 (alignment + 4-wheel balancing combined)
- Symptoms of misalignment: Steering pulling, uneven tread wear (inner/outer edge)
- Ignored alignment → tyres last 30–50% less

TYRE ROTATION
- Every 10,000 km
- Pattern: Front-to-rear and side-to-side (cross rotation)
- Skipping rotation → uneven wear, premature replacement

TYRE COST PER SET (4 tyres + fitting + balancing, May 2026)
- Hatchback (155–175 mm width):           Rs. 14,000 – 28,000
- Compact sedan (175–195 mm):              Rs. 18,000 – 38,000
- Compact SUV (205–215 mm):                Rs. 26,000 – 56,000
- Mid-size SUV (225–235 mm):               Rs. 36,000 – 72,000
- Full-size SUV (255–265 mm):              Rs. 56,000 – 1,20,000
- Luxury (run-flat / staggered):           Rs. 80,000 – 2,50,000

PUNCTURE & FLAT TYRE COSTS
- Tubeless puncture repair: Rs. 150 – 400
- Sidewall damage = replacement only (not repairable)
- Rim damage on pothole: Rs. 2,500 – 12,000 per rim

=======================================================================
SECTION 3 — BRAKES
=======================================================================

BRAKE PAD REPLACEMENT INTERVAL
- City driving (heavy stop-and-go):                25,000 – 35,000 km
- Mixed city/highway:                              35,000 – 45,000 km
- Highway-heavy use:                               45,000 – 65,000 km
- Aggressive / sporty driving:                     20,000 – 30,000 km
- CarTrust estimation benchmark:                   30,000 km per set

BRAKE DISC / ROTOR
- Replace every 2nd or 3rd pad change (60,000 – 100,000 km)
- Warped disc symptoms: Steering wheel shudder when braking from 80+ kmph
- Re-machining (skimming) only viable once

BRAKE FLUID
- Flush and replace: Every 2 years or 40,000 km (whichever first)
- Hygroscopic — absorbs moisture → reduced boiling point → brake fade
- Ignored → spongy pedal, fade on long descents, ABS issues

BRAKE PAD COST (per axle, at ASC)
- Hatchback front pads:                    Rs. 1,800 – 3,500
- Sedan / Compact SUV front pads:           Rs. 2,500 – 5,500
- Mid/Full-size SUV front pads:             Rs. 4,500 – 9,500
- Luxury front pads (BMW / Mercedes):       Rs. 12,000 – 35,000
- Rear pads (drum or disc): 50–70% of front cost
- Disc replacement: 2x pad cost typically

WARNING SIGNS THAT NEED IMMEDIATE ATTENTION
- Squealing during braking → pads at wear indicator (replace within 1,000 km)
- Grinding noise → pads worn to metal backing (rotor damage imminent)
- Pulling to one side → seized caliper (Rs. 4,000–12,000 per caliper)
- Spongy pedal → air in lines or fluid contamination (bleed required)
- ABS warning light → sensor / module fault (Rs. 5,000–40,000)
- Handbrake / parking brake not holding → cable stretched or rear pads worn

=======================================================================
SECTION 4 — SUSPENSION, STEERING & SHOCKS
=======================================================================

SHOCK ABSORBER / STRUT LIFE
- Indian road conditions: 60,000 – 90,000 km (front)
- Rear shocks: 80,000 – 1,20,000 km
- Symptoms of failure: Bouncy ride, dive on braking, knocking over speed breakers,
  oily wetness on shock body
- Cost (pair): Rs. 4,500 – 12,000 (hatch) / Rs. 8,000 – 25,000 (SUV) /
               Rs. 25,000 – 80,000 (luxury)

LOWER ARM BUSHES / BALL JOINTS
- Typical life: 60,000 – 80,000 km
- Symptoms: Clunking over bumps, looseness in steering, vibration
- Cost: Rs. 2,500 – 8,000 per side

WHEEL BEARINGS
- Typical life: 80,000 – 1,20,000 km
- Symptoms: Humming/droning that changes with speed, growling on turns
- Cost: Rs. 3,500 – 9,000 per wheel (mainstream); Rs. 18,000 – 45,000 (luxury)

POWER STEERING
- Hydraulic fluid flush: Every 60,000 km / 4 years
- Electric power steering (EPS): Sealed, only fault diagnosis if heavy
- Rack failure: Rs. 18,000 – 1,20,000 depending on model

STEERING SYMPTOMS BY SEVERITY
- Slight play in straight line → tie rod end wear (Rs. 1,200 – 3,000)
- Vibration at 80–100 kmph → wheel balancing or worn shocks
- Constant pulling → alignment or one-side caliper seize
- Heavy steering at low speed → EPS module / hydraulic pump failure
- Clunk on turning → CV joint or steering column fault (Rs. 6,000 – 25,000)

=======================================================================
SECTION 5 — TIMING BELT / CHAIN
=======================================================================

TIMING BELT REPLACEMENT (rubber belt — non-Maruti petrol, older Ford/Skoda)
- Interval: Every 80,000 – 1,00,000 km OR 5–6 years (whichever first)
- Replace water pump and tensioner pulleys at the same time
- Failure = bent valves / piston damage = catastrophic engine repair
- Cost (belt + pulleys + water pump): Rs. 8,000 – 30,000
- Cars known for timing belt: Older VW/Skoda 1.6 MPI, older Ford EcoSport diesel

TIMING CHAIN (most modern engines)
- Chain itself: Lifetime in theory (1.5–2 lakh km)
- Tensioner / guides: 1.5 lakh km — many engines have known weak spots
- Symptoms of stretched chain: Rattle on cold start, "check engine" with VVT codes
- Cost to fix stretched chain: Rs. 35,000 – 1,50,000 (engine top end strip-down)

ENGINES WITH KNOWN TIMING CHAIN ISSUES (Indian market)
- Hyundai Kappa 1.0L (early Grand i10): Chain rattle 80k+ km
- VW/Skoda 1.4 TSI EA111 (Polo GT / Vento TSI): Chain failure 60–90k km
- BMW N20 (320i pre-2015): Timing chain weakness, costly to fix
- Mercedes M271 (older C-class): Chain stretches at high mileage
- Audi 2.0 TFSI EA888 Gen 1: Tensioner wear; check service records

=======================================================================
SECTION 6 — COOLANT, RADIATOR & THERMAL MANAGEMENT
=======================================================================

COOLANT FLUSH INTERVAL
- Standard (green / yellow): Every 40,000 km or 2 years
- Long-life (OEM red / pink, e.g. G12+ for VW group): Every 80,000 km or 5 years
- Indian climate accelerates degradation — flush sooner if discoloured
- Cost: Rs. 1,800 – 4,500 (mainstream); Rs. 4,500 – 12,000 (luxury)

RADIATOR INSPECTION
- Visible leaks → replace (Rs. 4,000 – 25,000)
- Bent fins from stone strike → re-core if cooling efficiency drops
- Cooling fan motor failure: Common at 1 lakh km (Rs. 4,000 – 18,000)

RED FLAGS — COOLING SYSTEM
- White exhaust smoke (not condensation) → head gasket failure
- Coolant level dropping with no visible leak → internal leak (very bad)
- Sweet smell from engine bay → coolant leak somewhere
- Overheating in traffic but OK on highway → fan / fan motor failure
- Heater not warming up → thermostat stuck open or low coolant
- Engine takes >5 min to warm up → thermostat stuck open (Rs. 1,200–4,500)

OVERHEATING DAMAGE COST PROGRESSION
- One mild overheat → potentially nothing
- Multiple overheats / driven hot → head gasket failure (Rs. 25,000 – 80,000)
- Severe overheat → warped head + gasket + possible block damage
  (Rs. 80,000 – 2,50,000 = often write-off for older cars)

=======================================================================
SECTION 7 — TRANSMISSION (Manual, AMT, CVT, DCT, TC AT)
=======================================================================

MANUAL TRANSMISSION
- Gearbox oil change: Every 40,000 – 60,000 km
- Clutch life (city): 60,000 – 80,000 km
- Clutch life (highway): 80,000 – 1,20,000 km
- Cost (gear oil change): Rs. 1,500 – 4,000
- Cost (clutch replacement): Rs. 8,000 – 22,000 mainstream; Rs. 25,000 – 80,000 luxury

CLUTCH FAILURE SYMPTOMS
- High biting point → clutch plate worn
- Slipping under load (RPM rises without acceleration) → replace immediately
- Judder on engagement → flywheel surface needs machining
- Hard pedal / no return → master/slave cylinder failure (Rs. 3,500 – 12,000)

AMT (AUTOMATED MANUAL — Celerio, Swift AMT, WagonR AMT, etc.)
- Same clutch as manual + actuator unit
- Actuator failure typical at 80,000 – 1,20,000 km (Rs. 25,000 – 60,000)
- Gear oil change every 40,000 km

CVT (HONDA CITY/AMAZE, HYUNDAI VERNA/CRETA IVT, KIA SELTOS IVT, NISSAN)
- Transmission fluid: Every 40,000 km (CRITICAL — often neglected)
- Belt/chain inside CVT cannot be repaired — only full unit replacement
- Cost of new CVT unit: Rs. 1,50,000 – 4,50,000
- Symptoms of damage: Whining, juddering at constant speed, lurching from stop
- BUYER WARNING: If service log has NO CVT fluid change at 40k/80k → factor
  in Rs. 6,000–10,000 for immediate flush, OR up to Rs. 4.5L if damaged

DCT / DSG (VW POLO/VENTO, TAIGUN, SKODA, KIA SELTOS DCT, HYUNDAI VENUE DCT)
- Mechatronic unit fluid change: Every 40,000 – 60,000 km
- Cost of fluid change: Rs. 8,000 – 18,000 (specialised oil + procedure)
- Known issues: VW DQ200 mechatronic failures (Rs. 1.5L – 3L)
- BS6 Kia/Hyundai 7-DCT (wet): Improved reliability; still service on schedule

TORQUE CONVERTER AT (HONDA CITY 5-AT, FORTUNER 6-AT, ENDEAVOUR 10-AT, BMW ZF 8-AT)
- ATF (Automatic Transmission Fluid) change: Every 60,000 – 80,000 km
- "Lifetime fluid" claim by some brands is misleading — ALWAYS change
- Cost (full ATF service): Rs. 10,000 – 35,000
- Symptoms of trouble: Harsh shifts, slipping between gears, delayed engagement

=======================================================================
SECTION 8 — AIR FILTER, FUEL FILTER, AC FILTER
=======================================================================

ENGINE AIR FILTER
- Replace: Every 20,000 km / 12 months
- Severe dust conditions (rural India / construction zones): 10,000 km
- Cost: Rs. 350 – 1,200 (paper); Rs. 1,500 – 4,500 (high-flow / luxury)

CABIN / AC FILTER
- Replace: Every 15,000 – 20,000 km / 12 months
- Symptoms of neglect: Weak airflow, musty smell, fogging windows
- Cost: Rs. 400 – 1,500 (mainstream); Rs. 1,800 – 6,000 (luxury / activated carbon)

FUEL FILTER
- Petrol: Every 40,000 – 60,000 km (often inside fuel tank assembly)
- Diesel: Every 20,000 – 40,000 km (CRITICAL for common-rail diesels)
- Cost: Rs. 800 – 4,500 (petrol); Rs. 1,500 – 6,500 (diesel)
- Neglected diesel filter → injector damage (Rs. 8,000 – 25,000 per injector)

DPF / DIESEL PARTICULATE FILTER (BS6 diesels — Creta, Harrier, etc.)
- Regenerates automatically every 200–500 km
- Failure = limp mode + DPF warning light
- City-only driving prevents regeneration → eventual clog
- Cost of DPF replacement: Rs. 70,000 – 2,50,000
- Forced regen at ASC: Rs. 3,500 – 9,000

ADBLUE / DEF (luxury & some BS6 diesels)
- Top up every 8,000 – 15,000 km (5–10 L tank)
- Cost: Rs. 800 – 2,500 per top-up (DIY) / Rs. 2,500 – 5,000 (ASC)
- Tank/pump failures common (Rs. 35,000 – 1,20,000)

SECTION 9 — BATTERY & ELECTRICAL
=======================================================================

12V BATTERY
- Life expectancy: 3–4 years (Indian heat shortens this)
- Premium maintenance-free / AGM: 4–5 years
- Symptoms of failure: Slow crank, electrical glitches, AC compressor cycling
- Cost: Rs. 4,500 – 10,000 (hatch); Rs. 8,000 – 18,000 (SUV); Rs. 18,000 – 45,000 (luxury AGM)

ALTERNATOR / GENERATOR
- Life: 1.5 – 2 lakh km typical
- Symptoms: Battery warning light, dimming headlights at idle, electrical glitches
- Cost: Rs. 6,000 – 18,000 (mainstream rebuild); Rs. 18,000 – 60,000 (new luxury)

STARTER MOTOR
- Life: 1.2 – 2 lakh km
- Symptoms: Slow / no crank when battery is good
- Cost: Rs. 3,500 – 12,000 (mainstream); Rs. 18,000 – 45,000 (luxury)

LIGHTING & ELECTRONICS
- Halogen bulbs: 200 – 800 hours (Rs. 250 – 800 per pair)
- LED / xenon failures: Module fault (Rs. 4,500 – 35,000)
- Power window motor: Rs. 3,500 – 12,000 per window
- Central locking actuator: Rs. 1,800 – 6,500 per door
- ABS sensor: Rs. 1,500 – 8,500 per wheel
- Reverse parking sensor: Rs. 400 – 1,800 per sensor
- Reverse camera: Rs. 3,500 – 18,000

DASHBOARD WARNING LIGHTS — DO NOT BUY IF LIT
- Check engine light (steady or flashing)
- ABS / brake / airbag warning
- Oil pressure warning
- Coolant temperature warning
- Battery charging warning
NOTE: Sellers sometimes disconnect bulbs to hide warnings. Verify with
OBD2 scanner — should show ZERO stored / pending fault codes.

SECTION 10 — AIR CONDITIONING (Critical in Indian climate)
=======================================================================

AC SYSTEM SERVICE INTERVAL
- Refrigerant top-up: Every 2–3 years (R134a / R1234yf for newer cars)
- Full AC service (gas, oil, leak check): Every 3 years
- Cost: Rs. 1,500 – 4,500 (top-up); Rs. 4,500 – 12,000 (full service)

COMPRESSOR FAILURE (most expensive AC issue)
- Typical failure: 1.2 – 1.8 lakh km in Indian conditions
- Cost: Rs. 18,000 – 50,000 (mainstream); Rs. 60,000 – 2,50,000 (luxury)
- Symptoms: AC blowing warm, clutch chattering, unusual noise from compressor

CONDENSER (front-mounted, vulnerable to stone strikes)
- Common failure: Punctured by stones, salt corrosion
- Cost: Rs. 6,000 – 25,000 (mainstream); Rs. 30,000 – 90,000 (luxury)

COOLING COIL / EVAPORATOR
- Behind dashboard — labour-intensive replacement
- Cost (parts + 10-12 hours labour): Rs. 18,000 – 80,000

AC RED FLAGS WHEN BUYING USED
- AC takes >5 min to cool in 35°C ambient → low gas / compressor weakness
- Hissing sound when AC engages → expansion valve issue
- Musty smell despite filter change → evaporator mould / drain blockage
- Rattling/knocking when AC compressor engages → clutch failure imminent

SECTION 11 — EXHAUST SYSTEM
=======================================================================

CATALYTIC CONVERTER
- Life: 1.5 – 2.5 lakh km (longer for petrol, shorter for diesel)
- Theft target in some cities (precious metal recovery)
- Cost: Rs. 8,000 – 35,000 (mainstream); Rs. 35,000 – 2,50,000 (luxury)
- Failure signs: Check engine + rattling under car + reduced power

MUFFLER / SILENCER
- Life: 8 – 10 years (corrosion-limited, monsoon-prone areas shorter)
- Cost: Rs. 3,500 – 12,000

EXHAUST MANIFOLD CRACKING
- Common on older diesels (1.4 CRDi, 2.0 CRDi early models)
- Symptoms: Ticking noise on cold start, loss of low-end torque
- Cost: Rs. 8,000 – 25,000

=======================================================================
SECTION 12 — INJECTORS & FUEL SYSTEM (DIESEL ESPECIALLY)
=======================================================================

DIESEL INJECTORS (common-rail, BS4 and BS6)
- Life: 1 – 1.5 lakh km if filter maintained
- Premature failure: <80k km if filter neglected or fuel adulterated
- Cost: Rs. 8,000 – 25,000 per injector (4-cylinder = Rs. 35,000 – 1,00,000 set)
- Test: Diagnostic balance test at ASC (Rs. 1,500 – 3,500)

DIESEL PUMP (CP3 / CP4 high-pressure)
- Life: 1.2 – 2 lakh km
- CP4 pumps (some Ford / Hyundai diesels) known to fail catastrophically
- Cost: Rs. 35,000 – 1,80,000

PETROL FUEL INJECTORS
- Generally more robust than diesel
- Symptoms of dirty injectors: Rough idle, poor mileage, hesitation
- Injector cleaning service: Rs. 1,500 – 4,500
- Replacement: Rs. 3,500 – 12,000 per injector

CARBON BUILD-UP (Direct-injection petrol — TSI, T-GDi, BS6 turbo)
- Walnut blasting service at 80k+ km: Rs. 18,000 – 45,000
- Symptoms: Rough idle, misfires, reduced fuel economy
- Engines affected: VW/Skoda 1.0/1.4/1.5 TSI, Hyundai/Kia 1.4/1.5 T-GDi,
  Honda City Hybrid 1.5L

=======================================================================
SECTION 13 — TURBOCHARGER (Diesel & Modern Turbo Petrol)
=======================================================================

TURBO LIFE EXPECTANCY
- Diesel turbo (well-maintained): 1.5 – 2 lakh km
- Petrol turbo: 1.2 – 1.8 lakh km
- Abused turbo (no cool-down, contaminated oil): <80k km

TURBO FAILURE SIGNS
- Whistling / whining noise at acceleration → bearing wear
- Blue smoke under load → oil seal failure
- Loss of power + check engine → wastegate / VGT stuck
- Oil in intercooler pipes → seal failure (immediate replacement needed)

COST OF TURBO REPLACEMENT
- Mainstream diesel (Creta CRDi, Nexon 1.5D): Rs. 35,000 – 80,000
- Mid-size diesel (Harrier 2.0, XUV700): Rs. 60,000 – 1,20,000
- Premium / luxury: Rs. 1,50,000 – 4,50,000

TURBO CARE RULES
- 30-60 sec idle before shutoff after hard driving
- Use ONLY oil meeting OEM spec
- Change oil on time — turbo bearings depend on it

SECTION 14 — RUST, PAINT & BODY
=======================================================================

UNDERBODY RUSTING TIMELINE
- Coastal cities (Mumbai / Chennai / Goa): Visible rust at 3–5 years
- Salt-affected (Kolkata / Vizag): Visible rust at 4–6 years
- Dry climate (Delhi / Pune / Bangalore): Visible rust at 7–10 years
- Hill stations (heavy rain + cold): 4–7 years
- Anti-rust coating (Wax / 3M / Anti-rust treatment): Extends by 3–5 years

KEY RUST INSPECTION POINTS
- Rear wheel arches (most common) — peek behind splash guard
- Door bottoms (inner edges from outside)
- Boot floor (lift carpet)
- Spare tyre well (lift mat)
- Sill panels (under doors)
- Front strut towers (open bonnet, look down)
- Cross-member under engine
- Brake lines along chassis

PAINT INSPECTION (DETECTING REPAINTS)
- Use paint depth gauge: Factory paint 80–150 microns; repaint 200–400 microns
- Orange peel texture differing between panels = repaint
- Overspray on rubber seals, plastic trims = panel-level repaint
- Different paint codes inside fuel flap vs B-pillar sticker = body repair
- Panel gap inconsistency = accident repair

WINDSHIELD & GLASS
- Crack in driver's line of sight = MUST replace (legal & safety)
- Stone-chip repair: Rs. 1,500 – 4,500 (only if not in line of sight)
- Full windshield replacement: Rs. 6,000 – 25,000 mainstream;
                                Rs. 35,000 – 1,20,000 luxury (rain sensor / HUD)
- Side glass: Rs. 2,500 – 12,000 per pane

=======================================================================
SECTION 15 — INTERIOR & UPHOLSTERY
=======================================================================

UPHOLSTERY WEAR INDICATORS
- Driver's seat bolster wear (left side of seat for India): Reflects ownership use
- Steering wheel wear vs claimed mileage: Heavy wear at <50k = rollback signal
- Pedal rubber wear: Smooth/worn pedals at low km = rollback signal
- Floor mat under driver: Worn patches = consistent with driving distance

DASHBOARD CRACKS / SUN DAMAGE
- Common in 5+ year old cars in north India / Rajasthan / Gujarat
- Repair: Re-laminate (Rs. 4,500 – 12,000) or replace pad (Rs. 25,000 – 60,000)

LEATHER SEAT MAINTENANCE
- Crack repair: Rs. 1,500 – 4,500 per seat
- Reupholstery (full set in leatherette): Rs. 18,000 – 45,000
- OEM leather replacement: Rs. 60,000 – 2,50,000

CARPET / FLOOR INSPECTION
- Lift floor mat → look for water staining, mud, mould
- Smell test: Musty / fungal smell = previous water ingress (flood?)
- Damp underlay = leaking sunroof / windshield seal / AC drain blockage

SECTION 16 — DRIVING CONDITIONS & USAGE PATTERNS
=======================================================================

AVERAGE ANNUAL KM DRIVEN — INDIA
- National average:                   10,000 – 12,000 km / year
- Urban metro (Mumbai / Delhi / BLR): 12,000 – 15,000 km / year
- Semi-urban (Tier 2):                 8,000 – 12,000 km / year
- Self-drive personal car (avg):       10,000 km / year
- Commercial / fleet:                  30,000 – 60,000 km / year
- CarTrust default:                    11,000 km / year

USAGE PATTERN ANALYSIS
- 1 owner, full ASC history, 11k/year: Premium private use
- 1–2 owners, mixed service, 15k+/year: Heavy private OR commercial
- 3+ owners, sparse service, 25k+/year: Likely fleet / commercial / Ola/Uber
- <8k/year average AND sat unused for periods: Risk of degraded seals, varnished
  fuel, dried rubber

INDICATORS OF COMMERCIAL / TAXI USE (avoid these)
- Yellow registration plate (commercial) — check RC carefully
- Multiple drivers' contact stickers inside
- Carpet very compressed in middle row (Innova / Ertiga / Carens)
- Dashboard / steering disproportionately worn
- Roof rust where light bar was mounted
- Distinct smell of multiple users (cleaning product overload)

=======================================================================
SECTION 17 — ODOMETER ROLLBACK DETECTION (CarTrust Multi-Signal)
=======================================================================

SIGNAL A — OIL CHANGE COUNT
- Stated_km vs oil_changes × 5,500 km
- If stated < expected by >20% → ROLLBACK SUSPICION

SIGNAL B — TYRE REPLACEMENTS
- Stated_km vs tyre_sets × 40,000 km
- 2 tyre sets but only 30k stated → ROLLBACK CONFIRMED

SIGNAL C — BRAKE PAD REPLACEMENTS
- Stated_km vs brake_sets × 30,000 km
- 2+ brake jobs but stated <50k → ROLLBACK SUSPICION

SIGNAL D — VEHICLE AGE
- Stated_km vs vehicle_age × 11,000 km
- 2018 car with 18k stated in 2026 → ROLLBACK SUSPICION (expected 80k+)

SIGNAL E — INSURANCE-DECLARED MILEAGE
- Compare across annual policy renewals
- Decreasing or stagnant mileage between years → ROLLBACK CONFIRMED

SIGNAL F — PHYSICAL WEAR CROSS-CHECK
- Pedal rubber smoothness, steering wheel polish, gear lever wear,
  seat bolster compression — must match stated km

CARTRUST RULE: Require 2+ independent signals to agree within 10% before
declaring odometer "verified clean". Otherwise mark "verified flagged" or
"critical" depending on disagreement magnitude.

SECTION 18 — TIMING-RELATED MAJOR SERVICES (SCHEDULED MILESTONES)
=======================================================================

20,000 KM SERVICE
- Engine oil + filter
- Air filter check / clean
- Cabin filter replace
- Brake fluid top up
- Tyre rotation + balance
- Wheel alignment
- Cost: Rs. 5,000 – 12,000

40,000 KM SERVICE (BIG SERVICE)
- Engine oil + filter
- Air + cabin + fuel filter replace
- Spark plugs (petrol) check / replace
- Coolant inspection
- Brake fluid flush
- Gearbox oil change (MT)
- Front brake pads (likely)
- Cost: Rs. 12,000 – 35,000

60,000 KM SERVICE
- Above + spark plugs replace (iridium spark plugs)
- Power steering fluid flush
- Tyre replacement likely
- Cost: Rs. 18,000 – 50,000

80,000 – 1,00,000 KM SERVICE (MAJOR MILESTONE)
- Above + timing belt + water pump (if belt-driven engine)
- ATF / CVT fluid change
- Coolant flush
- Rear brake pads
- Battery check / replace
- Cost: Rs. 30,000 – 1,20,000

1,20,000 KM SERVICE
- Above + tyre set 2
- Front shocks evaluation
- AC system service
- Cost: Rs. 25,000 – 70,000

1,50,000 KM SERVICE (RED-FLAG TERRITORY for cars still being daily-driven)
- Above + full underbody inspection
- Carbon clean (direct-injection petrol)
- Diesel injector inspection / cleaning
- Possible turbo / clutch nearing end of life

=======================================================================
SECTION 19 — FLOOD-DAMAGE DETECTION
=======================================================================

VISUAL & SENSORY CHECKS
- Lift floor mats and carpet — look for mud line / water stain on metal
- Open trunk, lift spare tyre — water stains, rust in spare well
- Open glove box — silt or moisture stains
- Check seat belt webbing all the way down — water line darker than top
- Look behind dash speakers / vents for silt
- Lift hood — silt in nooks, water lines on plastic
- Smell test: Persistent musty smell despite air freshener = floor wetness

ELECTRICAL TELLS OF FLOOD DAMAGE
- Cluster display dim or flickering
- Window motors slow or noisy
- Random electrical glitches
- Headlight condensation (was submerged)
- AC drain blocked → water inside cabin

INSURANCE & RECORD CHECKS
- Insurance claim coinciding with monsoon in a flood-prone city
- Claim with no body shop entry but high amount = total loss claim
- Multiple electrical-component claims in short window
- RC switched to different state after a flood event date

CARTRUST RULE FOR FLOOD CARS: Critical hard-stop. Even after "repair",
flood damage causes electrical failure for years. WALK AWAY.
